Shinnosuke Yoshida's homepage

I am a graduate student at the Graduate School of Environmental Science, Hokkaido University. I am interested in physical oceanography, Southern Ocean circulation, Antarctic Bottom Water formation, and climate dynamics.

My research uses idealized ocean models to investigate the dynamics of ocean circulation, mesoscale eddies, bottom topography, and meridional overturning circulation.

Research interests

My current research focuses on the role of ocean dynamics in the Southern Ocean and Antarctic climate system. In particular, I am interested in how eddies, topography, and surface buoyancy forcing control large-scale ocean circulation.

  • Southern Ocean circulation
  • Antarctic Circumpolar Current
  • Antarctic Bottom Water formation and feedback
  • Meridional overturning circulation
  • Mesoscale eddy energetics
  • Ocean modeling with MITgcm

Current projects

Energetics of the Antarctic Circumpolar Current

This project examines how rough bottom topography affects the energetics of the Antarctic Circumpolar Current. I focus on the relationship among isopycnal slopes, eddy kinetic energy, baroclinic conversion, and wind-driven momentum transport.

Antarctic Bottom Water feedback

This project investigates how changes in Antarctic Bottom Water formation can modify the meridional overturning circulation and heat transport toward the Antarctic region.
