About

About me

I am a graduate student at the Graduate School of Environmental Science, Hokkaido University. My research interests lie in physical oceanography, Southern Ocean circulation, Antarctic Bottom Water formation, and climate dynamics.

I use idealized ocean models to investigate how ocean circulation responds to changes in forcing, topography, eddy activity, and buoyancy processes.

Research

Research interests

Antarctic Bottom Water feedback

Investigating how reduced Antarctic Bottom Water formation can alter lower and upper overturning circulation and affect heat transport toward Antarctica.

Southern Ocean circulation

Studying the dynamics of the Antarctic Circumpolar Current and its role in connecting wind forcing, eddies, and meridional overturning circulation.

Mesoscale eddy energetics

Examining how mesoscale eddies interact with mean flows, topography, and buoyancy forcing through energy conversion processes.

Ocean modeling

Using MITgcm and idealized numerical experiments to isolate mechanisms controlling ocean circulation and climate feedbacks.
